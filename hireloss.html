Why Does Hair Loss Happen?

Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors. Some common causes include:

  • Genetics: Hereditary factors are the most common cause.
  • Hormonal Changes: Pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid imbalances.
  • Stress: Physical or emotional stress can result in temporary hair loss.
  • Nutrition: Poor diet or deficiencies can affect hair health.
  • Topical Treatments: Minoxidil (Rogaine) helps stimulate growth.
  • Medical Conditions: Autoimmune diseases, scalp infections, etc.

Tip: Consult a healthcare professional if you're experiencing sudden hair loss.

How to Address Hair Loss

There are several treatments available to manage hair loss:

  • Topical Treatments: Minoxidil (Rogaine) helps stimulate growth.
  • Prescription Medications: Finasteride (Propecia) blocks the hormone causing loss.
  • Hair Transplants: Follicular unit transplantation (FUT) or extraction (FUE) can restore hair.
  • Laser Therapy: Low-level laser devices may improve hair density.
  • Lifestyle Adjustments: Diet, stress management, and proper care can help.

Tip: Early intervention often leads to better results.
